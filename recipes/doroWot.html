Doro Wot Recipe

Description

Doro Wat is often considered the national dish of Ethiopia and is a staple at holiday celebrations and family gatherings. This slow-cooked stew is famous for its rich, mahogany-colored sauce made from a base of caramelized onions, fragrant garlic, ginger, and a spicy blend known as berbere. The chicken pieces are simmered until they are incredibly tender, absorbing the bold heat and earthy flavors of the spices

Traditionally, the dish is served with hard-boiled eggs that are added at the end of the cooking process to soak up the savory sauce. For the most authentic experience, Doro Wat is enjoyed with injera, a sourdough flatbread that acts as both a plate and a utensil to scoop up every last drop of the stew.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken drumsticks or thighs, skin removed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons berbere spice mix
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, melt the niter kibbeh or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they are deeply caramelized and golden brown, about 20-30 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the pot and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the berbere spice mix and cook for 1-2 minutes to toast the spices, being careful not to burn them.
  4. Add the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring to combine with the onions and spices.
  5. Add the chicken pieces to the pot, turning them to coat with the sauce. Cook for about 5 minutes until the chicken is lightly browned.
  6. Pour in the chicken broth, bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  7. If using, add the hard-boiled eggs to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking to allow them to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with injera or your choice of side.
