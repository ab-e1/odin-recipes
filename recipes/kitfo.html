Kitfo Recipe

Description

Kitfo is a traditional Ethiopian dish that consists of finely chopped raw beef, seasoned with a blend of spices and clarified butter. The beef is typically seasoned with mitmita, a spicy chili powder, and niter kibbeh, a fragrant Ethiopian spiced butter. The dish is often served with a side of ayib, a mild Ethiopian cheese, and gomen, a dish made from collard greens. Kitfo is a popular dish in Ethiopia, especially during special occasions and holidays. It is often enjoyed with injera, a sourdough flatbread that complements the rich flavors of the seasoned beef. The combination of the spicy, buttery beef with the mild cheese and greens creates a delicious and satisfying meal that is beloved by many.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lean beef, finely chopped or ground
  • 2 tablespoons niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
  • 1 tablespoon mitmita (Ethiopian chili powder) or to taste
  • Salt to taste
  • Ayib (Ethiopian cheese) and gomen (collard greens) for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the finely chopped or ground beef with the niter kibbeh and mitmita. Mix well to ensure the beef is evenly coated with the spices and butter. Season with salt to taste.
  2. Cover the bowl and let the mixture marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  3. Kitfo is traditionally served raw, but if you prefer it cooked, you can lightly sauté the mixture in a skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes until it is just cooked through, being careful not to overcook it as it should remain tender.
  4. Serve the kitfo with ayib and gomen on the side, along with injera or your choice of bread.
